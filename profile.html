Research

I work primarily in the area of forensic speech science. My main research interests include forensic phonetics, sociolinguistics and bilingualism.

My current PhD project centres around the interaction between bilingualism and forensic voice comparison. I study how the identifiability (or differentiability) of voices is affected by changes in the language spoken and the composition of the speech community. Previously, my MSc dissertation explored the acoustics of filled pauses (uh's and um's) by bilingual speakers and their implications on forensic voice comparison.

I am also interested in the sociophonetic aspects of varieties of English, particularly those in bilingual communities. During my MSc, I have worked on the rhythmic properties of Punjabi English.
