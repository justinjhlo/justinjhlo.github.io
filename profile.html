Research

My research interests lie broadly within phonetics and sociolinguistics. My current postdoctoral work focuses on the role of individuals and phonology on the production and perception of coarticulatory dynamics.

My previous work has mostly been situated specifically in the areas of forensic speech science and bilingualism. My PhD project centres around the issues of bilingualism in forensic voice comparison, where I study the discriminatory performance of speech features in cross-language voice comparisons. Previously, my MSc dissertation explored the acoustic characteristics of filled pauses (uh's and um's) by bilingual speakers and the implications for forensic voice comparison.

I am also interested in the sociophonetic aspects of varieties of English, particularly those in bilingual communities. During my MSc, I have worked on the rhythmic properties of Punjabi English.
