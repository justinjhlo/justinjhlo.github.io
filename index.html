Justin J. H. Lo

I am a postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Dynamics of the Articulation, Acoustics, and Processing of Speech (DAAPS) Lab at the Department of Speech, Hearing and Phonetic Sciences, University College London working on the DFG-AHRC funded project Speakers, Listeners, Languages: Patterns Of Variability And Contrast In Spoken Language Dynamics.

My research interests are in the general areas of phonetics and sociolinguistics. My doctoral thesis (University of York, viva pending) lies at the specific intersection of forensic speech science and bilingualism and focuses on Issues of Bilingualism in Likelihood Ratio-Based Forensic Voice Comparison.

Outside work, I spend most time solving crosswords, living in fictional worlds and struggling to follow baking recipes.
